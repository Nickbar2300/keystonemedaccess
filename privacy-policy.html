Section 01
Introduction

Keystone Health Partners ("Keystone," "we," "us," or "our") operates Keystone MedAccess, a HIPAA-compliant platform that enables authorized law firms to request and receive Medicare claims data for their clients who are Medicare beneficiaries, in connection with legal proceedings including personal injury, workers' compensation, and Social Security Disability cases.

This Privacy Policy describes our practices regarding the collection, use, storage, disclosure, and protection of information — including Protected Health Information (PHI) as defined under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA) — in connection with the Keystone MedAccess platform.

By using Keystone MedAccess, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, you should not use the platform.

Section 02
Who We Are

Keystone Health Partners is a Pennsylvania-based company operating as a HIPAA Business Associate. We provide technology services to licensed law firms (our "Clients") that facilitate the authorized retrieval of Medicare beneficiary data from the CMS Blue Button 2.0 API on behalf of those firms' clients (Medicare beneficiaries, or "Beneficiaries").

We are not a covered entity under HIPAA (we are not a healthcare provider, health plan, or healthcare clearinghouse), but we handle PHI on behalf of covered entities and their business associates and are therefore subject to HIPAA's Business Associate requirements.

Section 03
Information We Collect
3.1 Information from Law Firm Users

When a law firm registers for and uses Keystone MedAccess, we collect:

  • Firm name, address, and contact information
  • Names, email addresses, and job titles of firm personnel who access the platform
  • Authentication credentials managed via Microsoft Entra ID (we do not store passwords)
  • Case reference numbers and matter descriptions entered by firm users
  • Audit logs of all platform activity including logins, record requests, record views, and downloads
3.2 Information About Medicare Beneficiaries

When a law firm initiates a record request for a Medicare beneficiary, we collect:

  • Patient name, date of birth, and Medicare Beneficiary Identifier (MBI) as provided by the law firm
  • Identity verification result from our third-party identity verification provider
  • CMS OAuth 2.0 authorization tokens (stored encrypted; never exposed to firm users)
  • Medicare claims data retrieved via the CMS Blue Button 2.0 API upon explicit patient authorization, including: demographic information, Medicare coverage details (Parts A, B, D), Explanation of Benefits (claims data), and Part D prescription drug claims
3.3 Automatically Collected Information

We automatically collect limited technical data for security and operational purposes:

  • IP addresses and device information for security monitoring
  • Access logs and API call records for audit compliance
  • Session metadata (login times, duration, actions taken)

We do not use cookies for advertising, behavioral tracking, or any purpose beyond essential platform security and session management.

Section 05
Medicare Data & CMS Blue Button 2.0

Keystone MedAccess retrieves Medicare beneficiary data exclusively through the CMS Blue Button 2.0 API, a federal system operated by the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS). The following principles govern all Medicare data access:

Patient-Directed Authorization Required

No Medicare data is ever accessed without the beneficiary's explicit authorization. When a record request is initiated, the beneficiary receives a secure authorization link via email. This link directs them to Medicare.gov, where they independently authorize Keystone MedAccess to access their data. This authorization flow is operated entirely by CMS — Keystone Health Partners never handles Medicare.gov credentials.

Scope of Data Access

With the beneficiary's authorization, we access the following FHIR R4 data resources:

  • Patient: Name, date of birth, address, Medicare ID
  • Coverage: Medicare Part A, B, and D enrollment and coverage periods
  • ExplanationOfBenefit: Claims data including providers, diagnoses, procedures, and amounts
  • MedicationRequest: Part D prescription drug claims
Revocation

Beneficiaries may revoke their authorization at any time via Medicare.gov. Upon revocation, Keystone MedAccess immediately ceases any further data access. Previously retrieved records are retained only for the duration required to fulfill the legal matter for which access was authorized, then deleted per our retention schedule.

Section 06
Data Sharing & Disclosure
With Law Firm Clients

Medicare claims data retrieved for a beneficiary is shared exclusively with the law firm that initiated the authorized request, solely for the legal matter specified in the request. Each law firm must execute a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) with Keystone Health Partners before accessing any PHI.

With Service Providers (Subprocessors)

We share limited data with the following categories of service providers who assist in operating the platform, all of whom have executed appropriate data processing agreements and/or BAAs:

  • Microsoft Azure: Cloud infrastructure, encrypted storage, audit logging (HIPAA BAA in place)
  • Identity Verification Provider (CLEAR): Patient identity verification prior to data access (commercial agreement in place)
  • Microsoft Exchange Online: Sending patient authorization emails (HIPAA BAA in place)
Required Disclosures

We may disclose information if required by law, court order, or regulatory authority, including to the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) in connection with HIPAA compliance reviews or breach investigations. We will notify affected parties to the extent permitted by law.

We Will Never
  • Sell beneficiary PHI to any third party
  • Share beneficiary data with advertisers, data brokers, or analytics companies
  • Use beneficiary data to target individuals with marketing communications
  • Disclose PHI for purposes beyond the authorized legal matter without additional explicit authorization
Section 07
Security Safeguards

Keystone Health Partners implements the following technical and administrative safeguards to protect information, consistent with HIPAA Security Rule requirements:

Technical Controls
  • All data transmitted using TLS 1.3 encryption — no unencrypted channels
  • PHI at rest encrypted using AES-256 with Customer Managed Keys via Azure Key Vault
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) required for all platform users
  • Role-based access control limiting PHI access to minimum necessary
  • Immutable audit logging of all PHI access events retained for 7 years
  • Private network endpoints — database and storage layers have no public internet exposure
  • Short-lived presigned download URLs (15-minute expiry) — no persistent direct links to PHI
Administrative Controls
  • HIPAA Risk Analysis conducted prior to production launch
  • Workforce training on PHI handling and HIPAA requirements
  • BAAs executed with all service providers and law firm clients
  • Documented breach notification and incident response procedures
  • Third-party penetration testing prior to production launch

Despite these measures, no system is completely immune to security risks. In the event of a breach involving your PHI, we will notify affected individuals and HHS within the timeframes required by the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule.

Section 08
Data Retention

We retain information only as long as necessary for the purposes described in this policy and as required by law:

  • Medicare PHI (claims records, FHIR data): Retained for the duration of the active legal matter plus a minimum of 6 years as required by HIPAA, or longer if required by applicable state law. Records are deleted upon written request from the law firm at matter close, subject to legal hold requirements.
  • Audit logs: Retained for 7 years per HIPAA audit control requirements, stored separately from PHI.
  • Firm user account data: Retained for the duration of the firm's agreement with Keystone Health Partners, plus 3 years.
  • Identity verification records: Retained for 6 years to support audit trail requirements.

Automated Azure Blob Storage lifecycle policies enforce deletion schedules. Manual deletion requests are honored within 30 days unless a legal hold is in effect.

Section 09
Your Rights
Rights of Medicare Beneficiaries
  • Right to Refuse: You may decline to authorize data access. Refusal has no consequence with respect to your Medicare benefits.
  • Right to Revoke: You may revoke authorization at any time via Medicare.gov. Revocation is effective immediately for future access.
  • Right to Access: You may request a copy of the PHI Keystone Health Partners holds about you by contacting us at the address below.
  • Right to Correction: You may request correction of inaccurate PHI in our records.
  • Right to an Accounting: You may request a list of disclosures of your PHI made by Keystone Health Partners.
  • Right to Deletion: You may request deletion of your records from our systems; requests are honored within 30 days absent a legal hold.
  • Right to Complain: If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with the HHS Office for Civil Rights at hhs.gov/ocr or by calling 1-800-368-1019. We will not retaliate against you for filing a complaint.
Rights of Law Firm Users
  • You may request access to, correction of, or deletion of your account data by contacting nbarbera@keystonehealthpartners.com.
  • Data deletion requests will be processed within 30 days, subject to legal retention requirements.

Section 11
Children's Privacy

Keystone MedAccess is designed for use by licensed legal professionals and adult Medicare beneficiaries. We do not knowingly collect personal information from individuals under the age of 18. Medicare eligibility generally begins at age 65 or upon qualifying disability, making our user base exclusively adult.

If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected information from a minor, we will delete it promptly.
