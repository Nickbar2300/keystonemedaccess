Section 02
Description of Services

Keystone MedAccess provides authorized law firms with a secure, HIPAA-compliant platform to:

  • Initiate requests for Medicare claims data on behalf of their clients (Medicare beneficiaries) who provide explicit authorization
  • Verify patient identity prior to initiating data requests via an integrated identity verification service
  • Retrieve Medicare beneficiary data (Part A, B, and D claims) via the CMS Blue Button 2.0 API following patient authorization
  • View, download, and share structured attorney-ready reports and PDF summaries of Medicare claims data
  • Maintain a secure, auditable record of all data requests and retrievals

Keystone Health Partners is a technology platform provider and is not a law firm, healthcare provider, or legal advisor. Nothing in our platform or these Terms constitutes legal, medical, or professional advice.

Section 05
CMS Blue Button 2.0 & Patient Authorization
⚠ CRITICAL: Medicare beneficiary data is retrieved exclusively through the CMS Blue Button 2.0 API and only with explicit patient authorization. No data may be accessed, requested, or retrieved without the beneficiary independently completing the CMS OAuth 2.0 authorization flow. Any attempt to circumvent this requirement is a material breach of these Terms and a potential HIPAA violation.
5.1 Patient Authorization Requirement

Every record request through Keystone MedAccess requires the Medicare beneficiary to independently authorize access to their data by completing the CMS Blue Button 2.0 OAuth 2.0 authorization flow on Medicare.gov. This authorization cannot be completed by your firm on behalf of the beneficiary under any circumstances.

5.2 CMS Terms of Use

Your firm's use of Medicare data obtained via Keystone MedAccess is subject to the CMS Blue Button 2.0 Terms of Use, available at bluebutton.cms.gov/terms. By using our platform, you agree to comply with all applicable CMS terms, policies, and applicable federal law governing Medicare data.

5.3 No Coercion of Beneficiaries

Your firm agrees not to pressure, coerce, or incentivize Medicare beneficiaries to authorize data access. Authorization must be freely and voluntarily given by the beneficiary after receiving a plain-language explanation of the purpose of the request.

5.4 Revocation

Beneficiaries may revoke their authorization at any time via Medicare.gov. Upon revocation, your firm must immediately cease any further use of previously retrieved data except as required to complete the specific legal matter for which authorization was obtained.
